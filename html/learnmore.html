Input meshes:

-24 scanned STL (image segmentation) meshes from medical imaging for diastole
-24 scanned STL (image segmentation) meshes from medical imaging for systole
-Same patients are used for systole and diastole
-Patients were all monitored for ATAA but used geometries are not especially preoperatory ones

Statistical Shape Analysis input meshes:

-Feature aligned isotopological analogous pure quadrilateral meshes (24 diastole + 24 systole)
-See: 3D volumetric isotopological meshing for finite element and isogeometric based reduced order modeling, Computer Methods in Applied Mechanics and Engineering, T. Maquart et al., Volume 362, 15 April 2020
-See: Trivariate Models Generation From Unstructured Surface Manifolds For Isogeometric Analysis - Application To Reduced Order Modeling With Geometric Parameters, PhD Thesis, T. Maquart, INSA-Lyon, 2019

Statistical Shape Analysis:

-Shown results are based on a smart SVD (Singular Value Decomposition) using Eigen 3.2.9
-All input meshes have been moved in the same basis in order to avoid rigid body modes
-All arterial branches of input meshes have been cutted at the same anatomical points

Remarks:

-Few generated quadrilateral meshes can show some unwanted parameterization artefacts, e.g. a minor parameterization twist or a slight curvature breakage near boundaries
-All patients are subject to ATAA but some of them are also subject to DTAA (Descending Thoracic Aortic Aneurysm)
-Extrapolation, i.e. evaluating statistical geometries out the space of geometrical parameters is not permitted in the web version. It can yield severe mesh distortion or unlikely geometries
